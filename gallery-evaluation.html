This self-evaluation essay provides me with an opportunity to assess the steps I have followed in accomplishing my tasks. It also allows me to reflect on the skills and techniques I have employed throughout the process, as well as the difficulties encountered while using the chosen tools and equipment to create the artwork. This evaluation will be instrumental in helping me identify my strengths and weaknesses, thereby understanding what and how to improve.

The first task assigned to me involved selecting a color scheme from a specific era and creating a collection of artworks. To begin, I conducted research on various art styles and color schemes from the mentioned eras, utilizing resources such as Pinterest and Google. This research led to the creation of a mood board using a selection of stock images to represent the chosen color scheme. Following this, I produced a variety of sketches, which later served as the foundation for the artwork's structure. These sketches were then organized into three distinct folders labeled ‘raster’, ‘vector’, and ‘not using’. In parallel, I compiled a database of stock images to assist in the creation of the artworks.

Initially, I planned to create raster images using Affinity Photo and the XP Artist 13.3 Pro graphic tablet. However, this process proved to be quite tedious and unsatisfying, primarily due to the low quality of the output and the significant time consumption. Consequently, I switched to using Procreate on the iPad for raster image creation.

This involved placing stock images onto the canvas, editing them, and arranging them according to the chosen sketch. From these arranged images, I created a second sketch. This second sketch was then lowered in opacity to facilitate the drawing of clean, clear line art over it.

Vector images were created using Affinity Designer, alongside the XP Artist 13.3 Pro graphic tablet. The process began with placing stock images onto the canvas. These images served as a reference for creating specific shapes using the pen tool, which were then utilized to closely replicate the structure of the chosen sketch. After establishing the main elements, the background was designed to complement the overall composition. Special effects were then applied to enhance the visual appeal of the artwork. Finally, the completed vector artwork was exported in the EPS format.

The second task involved creating a gallery that complements the artworks I had previously created. The initial concept for the gallery was a carousel-like design. I started by sketching the wireframe by hand in KeepNotes, and then created a cleaner version in Figma. However, this carousel concept had to be abandoned because Acrobat Reader no longer supports the 'object state' feature following the retirement of Flash. As a result, I redesigned the wireframes, this time in the form of a zine.

The gallery creation process initially utilized Adobe InDesign. However, upon completion and during my attempt to export it as an interactive PDF, I encountered a major issue: the chosen interactive elements were not functioning correctly. It appeared that these features might no longer be supported. Consequently, I decided to abandon this approach. Working with InDesign proved to be a highly challenging experience for me. I found the software to be tiresome, the learning curve steep, and the control mechanisms less intuitive compared to other applications I have used in the past.

I later opted to use Affinity Publisher for the creation of the gallery. The process began with the design of the background, which featured a gradient embellished with stars. Following this, I created the title in an elliptical form to add to the visual appeal. After finalizing the background and the title, these elements were exported as PNG files. Subsequently, the artworks were strategically placed along with these elements throughout the gallery.

Upon completion of the gallery layout, I inserted hyperlinks onto the buttons. The final step was to export the entire gallery as a PDF file.

Personally, I found most of the tasks to be quite enjoyable, particularly the creation of the artwork. Reflecting on the completed project, I am very satisfied with the outcome of both the gallery and the artwork. It was a fulfilling experience, and I would be eager to undertake similar projects again if the opportunity presents itself.
